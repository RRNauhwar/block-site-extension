Block lists

Create unlimited named lists (e.g. "Deep Work", "Study", "Evenings").

    Focus modes explained

    Soft

    Sites are blocked, but you can end the session anytime. Best for building the habit.

    Hard

    Ending early requires a 2-minute waiting period — long enough to break an impulse.

    Extreme 🔥

    No normal quitting. A "recovery unlock" exists but takes 10 minutes to complete, deliberately defeating impulsive exits. Each Extreme session also generates a private recovery code stored in your local history.

    AI accountability coach

    The built-in coach is rule-based and runs fully offline using your real in-session behaviour. Optionally connect your own AI model below for richer, generated coaching.

    Connect your own AI model (optional, advanced)

    Your key is stored only in local extension storage on this device and is sent solely to the endpoint you specify. Leave disabled to stay 100% offline.

    Cross-device sync

    Uses Chrome's built-in storage.sync (tied to your Google profile). Your analytics history stays local. Your AI key is never synced.

    Privacy

    • No accounts, no servers, no analytics SDKs, no tracking.
    • All sessions, attempts and stats live in chrome.storage.local on your device.
    • Optional sync uses Chrome's own encrypted sync — we never see your data.
    • Privacy is a feature, not a setting we can revoke.

    Stronger enforcement (strict mode)

    A browser extension cannot block desktop/mobile apps or stop its own removal. For tamper-resistant enforcement, combine Focus Lock AI with OS- or organization-level controls.
    1. Force-install via policy. Chrome's ExtensionInstallForcelist + ExtensionSettings (force_installed) prevent removal/disabling.
    2. Managed Chrome / Admin console. Pin the extension and block chrome://extensions.
    3. Restricted OS account. A non-admin user during focus hours can't change these policies.
    4. ChromeOS / Family Link. A supervisor can enforce policies the supervised user can't undo.

    App/notification blocking and uninstall protection require the native desktop & mobile apps on our roadmap (see ARCHITECTURE.md).
